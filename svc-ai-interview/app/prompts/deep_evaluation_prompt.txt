SCORING DIMENSIONS (0-10 scale each)

Score the candidate on each dimension using a 0-10 scale with decimal precision:

1. **technical_knowledge** (0-10): Depth and breadth of technical skills relevant to the role. Consider accuracy of answers, ability to discuss implementation details, understanding of underlying concepts.

2. **problem_solving** (0-10): Analytical thinking, ability to break down complex problems, structured approach to solutions, handling of edge cases and constraints.

3. **communication** (0-10): Clarity of expression, ability to explain technical concepts, structured responses, appropriate level of detail, active listening indicators.

4. **system_design** (0-10): Architectural thinking, ability to design scalable systems, understanding of trade-offs, awareness of distributed systems challenges. Score 5 if not assessed.

5. **coding_skill** (0-10): Code quality, correctness, efficiency, problem-solving approach in coding challenges. Score 5 if no coding was included.

6. **confidence** (0-10): Poise under pressure, ability to handle difficult questions, recovery from mistakes, willingness to say "I don't know" vs. bluffing.

---
ADDITIONAL ASSESSMENT DIMENSIONS

7. **domain_expertise** (0-10): Industry-specific knowledge, regulatory awareness, role-specific understanding.

8. **leadership_potential** (0-10): Initiative, decision-making, mentoring indicators, ownership mindset. Even for IC roles, assess growth trajectory.

9. **cultural_fit** (0-10): Values alignment, collaboration indicators, communication style compatibility, growth mindset.

10. **adaptability** (0-10): How well the candidate handled follow-up questions, unexpected topics, difficulty escalation, and pivots.

---
OVERALL SCORE CALCULATION

overall_score = weighted average:
- technical_knowledge: 25%
- problem_solving: 20%
- communication: 15%
- system_design: 15% (if assessed, otherwise redistribute)
- coding_skill: 10% (if assessed, otherwise redistribute)
- confidence: 5%
- domain_expertise: 5%
- cultural_fit: 5%

---
STRENGTHS & WEAKNESSES

Identify the candidate's top 3-5 strengths and top 3-5 weaknesses based on the interview. Be specific — reference actual answers and topics from the transcript.

---
RISK FLAGS

Identify any concerning patterns:
- Resume inconsistencies (claims vs. demonstrated knowledge)
- Signs of external help (long pauses followed by perfect, rehearsed answers)
- Inability to discuss claimed projects in depth
- Evasive or deflective answers on specific topics
- Pattern of bluffing (confident but incorrect answers)
- Integrity concerns from proctoring data

---
HIRE RECOMMENDATION

Based on overall_score and qualitative assessment:
- **strong_hire**: overall_score >= 8.0 AND no risk flags AND strong across all dimensions
- **hire**: overall_score >= 6.5 AND no critical risk flags
- **maybe**: overall_score 5.0-6.49 OR mixed signals OR notable concerns
- **no_hire**: overall_score < 5.0 OR critical risk flags OR major resume inconsistencies

---
RESUME VERIFICATION

Compare interview answers against resume claims:
- Were specific projects/technologies discussed consistent with what's on the resume?
- Did the candidate demonstrate genuine hands-on experience with claimed skills?
- Flag any claims that appeared exaggerated or inconsistent

---
ROUND-BY-ROUND SUMMARY

For each interview round, provide:
- Round type and topics covered
- Performance level (strong/adequate/weak)
- Key observations
- Notable answers (good or bad)

PROCTORING FACTOR

Consider the proctoring summary:
- If integrity_score < 70%, note potential integrity concerns
- Multiple long tab-away events during technical questions are more concerning
- Brief tab switches may be benign (notifications, etc.)
- Factor into risk_flags if significant
